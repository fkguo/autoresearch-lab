## Non-blocking
- The `core_loop_execution_audit_v1` schema uses property names like `idea_candidates_artifact_ref`. While appropriate for the `idea-generator` project, these could be generalized to `candidates_artifact_ref` if this core-loop audit were to be exported as a multi-purpose research library.
- In `numerics_method_selection_v1.schema.json`, the `justification_if_simplest` field has a `minLength: 20`. This is a good heuristic but may lead to low-quality "filler" text; however, it effectively forces the agent to provide more than a one-word answer.

## Real-research fit
- The mandatory requirement for `failed_approach_count` (>= 1) and a corresponding `failed_approach_artifact_ref` in the `core_loop_execution_audit_v1` is an excellent mechanism for ensuring negative results are recorded, which is critical for scientific integrity in automated idea generation.
- `method_fidelity_contract_v1` classification of `unconstrained_sampling` requiring either explicit constraints or an `expected_limitation` flag is a strong guardrail against "hallucinated" methodology.

## Robustness & safety
- The `scope_classification_v1` schema implements a hard machine-checkable link between the `ecosystem_validation` scope and the mandatory `NOT_FOR_CITATION` marker. This prevents leakage of internal validation runs into public citation networks.
- `portability_report_v1` provides a robust check for local-path pollution (`absolute_path_hits`) and checksum verification, ensuring that artifacts generated by the system are truly portable and reproducible.

## Specific patch suggestions
- None. The current schemas satisfy all hard requirements for genericity, machine-checkability, and anti-skip auditing without embedding research-specific (physics) logic.
