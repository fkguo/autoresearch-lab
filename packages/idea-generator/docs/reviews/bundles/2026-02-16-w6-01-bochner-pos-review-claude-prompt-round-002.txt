## A) Positivity Meaning in arXiv:2412.00848 (Resolved)

In `tranden_fin.tex` (arXiv:2412.00848), Sec. `Positivity`:
- They write $\Theta^{++}$ in light-front gauge $A^+=0$ as
  - a quark piece expressible via number operators weighted by $p^+$ after $\int dx^-$, and
  - a gluon piece $\Theta_g^{++}=(\partial^+ A_\perp^a)^2$ which is manifestly nonnegative.
- They derive a folding/convolution formula for wave packets:
  - Eq. `thetapp`: $\langle\psi|\int dx^- \Theta_q^{++}(b,x^-)|\psi\rangle = \int d^2b'\, n_\psi^+(b-b')\,A_q(b')$.
  - For sharply localized packets, $n_\psi^+(b)\to p^+\delta^{(2)}(b)$, giving $\langle\psi|\int dx^- \Theta^{++}(b,x^-)|\psi\rangle \to P^+ A(b)$.
- Since the operator is positive (number-operator structure), this implies **pointwise** $A(b)\ge 0$ for the pion’s transverse density.

We therefore treat $A(b)\ge 0$ as the executable constraint (enforced on a $b$ grid), consistent with the paper’s own phrasing “positivity condition $A(b)>0$” (see their Fig. caption around `fig:Ab`).

## B) Channel Identification: D0 $\leftrightarrow$ $A^\pi$ (Resolved)

The bound pipeline is explicitly **D-wave / $2^{++}$ / pion-only**:

- In the GTB seed paper (arXiv:2403.10772 LaTeX `arxiv_submission_3_v10.tex`), the D0 current is explicitly the light-front component
  - `j^0_2 = T^{++}(0)` (Eq. label `D0op`).
- In the dispersive pion GFF paper (arXiv:2507.05375 LaTeX `sn-article.tex`), the single-channel unitarity relation is
  - Eq. `eq.ImApi`: ${\rm Im}\,A^\pi(t) = \frac{2p_\pi}{\sqrt{t}}\,(t_2^0(t))^*\,A^\pi(t)$,
  and the text immediately notes that $A^\pi$ carries the well-defined $J^{PC}=2^{++}$ channel.

Thus, the D0 ($I=0$, $\ell=2$) channel is the correct single-channel object for constraining $A^\pi$ without coupled-channel inputs, and it is consistent to use the D0 envelope as an input guardrail for $|{\rm Im}\,A^\pi(s)|$ in this prototype.

## C) UV Tail Truncation Fix: pQCD tail from 2412 (New in Round 002)

Round-001 v1 enforced dispersion/sum rules and $A(b)\ge 0$ only on $s\in[4,s_0]$ (inherited from the envelope run). This made “bounds” ambiguous.

Round-002 adds an explicit UV tail model for ${\rm Im}\,A(s)$ taken from arXiv:2412.00848 Eq. `imF`:
$$
\\frac{1}{\\pi}{\\rm Im}\,f_p(s) = - c_f\\,\\frac{64\\pi^2 f_\\pi^2}{\\beta_0\\,s\\,(\\ln^2(s/\\Lambda^2)+\\pi^2)}.
$$
For $f=A$, the paper uses $c_A=3$ (and $\beta_0=9$, $\Lambda=225$ MeV). We implement this as a fixed tail contribution for $s\\ge s_{\\max}$ (where $s_{\\max}$ is the envelope grid maximum), with a configurable scale factor.
