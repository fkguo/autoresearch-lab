## Purpose

W6-21 identified ASR (the asymptotic sum rule) as a dominant “UV knob” controlling both $A_{\max}(-Q^2)$ and the implied low-energy slope floor $f_1^{\min}$.

W6-22 replaces the binary ASR on/off switch with a **generalized, auditable UV-band constraint**:
$$
\left|\frac{1}{\pi}\int ds\,{\rm Im}A(s)-{\rm asr\_target}\right|\le {\rm tol}_{\rm ASR},
$$
implemented as linear inequalities compatible with the conic program.

Goals of this packet:
1) confirm the **units/semantics** of the generalized ASR implementation (especially the $\pi$ conventions and tail subtraction),
2) validate the **implied-$f_1$ threshold scan** vs ${\rm tol}_{\rm ASR}$,
3) validate the **tightening claim** for $A^\pi(-Q^*)$ when combining (soft ASR band) + (low-energy slope input),
4) assess whether the required ${\rm tol}_{\rm ASR}$ size is “physically interpretable” or should be treated as a placeholder until anchored to a real UV/OPE estimate.

## Claims under review (W6-22)

1) **Correct semantics (units + tail subtraction)**  
   - ASR band target/tolerance are defined in the same “$f$-style” units as other sum rules: ${\rm asr\_target} = (1/\pi)\int ds\,{\rm Im}A(s)$.
   - Tail subtraction is handled consistently: the enforced band applies to the full integral (grid + fixed tail), and audit residuals reflect the shifted RHS.

2) **Implied-$f_1$ threshold scan is an appropriate feasibility diagnostic**  
   The implied-$f_1$ diagnostic under soft ASR correctly predicts feasibility of the slope-constrained runs.

3) **Tightening is real and auditable**  
   The softened ASR band + slope input produces a materially tighter $A^\pi(-Q^*)$ interval, with residual audits and cross-solver agreement at the “few $10^{-3}$” level.

4) **Interpretability risk**  
   The required ${\rm tol}_{\rm ASR}$ is large (order $10^2$ in the current normalization). This likely indicates:
   - ASR is too rigid as a UV input under the current tail model / cutoff, and/or
   - a better-motivated generalized UV/OPE input (nonzero RHS, bounded RHS, or tail-uncertainty model) is needed before physics interpretation.

## Questions for reviewers

1) Is the ASR band implementation correctly normalized and consistent with the existing $f_0,f_1$ sum-rule conventions?
2) Are we double-counting or miscounting the UV tail contributions in the ASR and slope constraints?
3) Does the implied-$f_1$ threshold scan provide a trustworthy feasibility predictor?
4) Does the tightening at ${\rm tol}_{\rm ASR}=62$ look robust enough to proceed to the next island (UV/OPE anchoring), or do we need additional numerical stability work first?
